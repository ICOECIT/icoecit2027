Publication status — under review, not yet approved

ICoECIT 2027 is being organized by G. Narayanamma Institute of Technology & Science (GNITS), Hyderabad, which is the financial sponsor of the conference. An application for IEEE technical co-sponsorship and publication of the proceedings is currently under review with IEEE and has not yet been approved. Until formal approval and a signed agreement are in place, no IEEE sponsorship should be assumed and the IEEE name/logo is not used to imply endorsement. The proceedings of the previous edition, ICoECIT 2026, were published by IEEE — see History.

About the Conference

A global platform for cutting-edge research

ICoECIT aims to bring together researchers, academicians, industry professionals and innovators to explore the frontiers of modern computing and intelligent systems, spanning artificial intelligence, machine learning, IoT, cybersecurity, embedded systems and smart electronics.

Present & Publish

Explore Frontiers

ICoECIT 2027 Theme

Candidate themes for the 2027 edition

A shortlist of catchy, on-brand themes proposed for the organizing committee to choose from. The first is our recommended lead theme.

★ Recommended

“Intelligent Horizons: Computing for a Connected Tomorrow”

Positions the conference around human-centred, connected intelligence — broad enough to welcome AI, IoT, networks and smart systems.

Theme 02

“Emerging Frontiers: Where Intelligence Meets Innovation”

Emphasises the “emerging” identity of ICoECIT and the fusion of intelligent methods with real-world innovation.

Theme 03

“Convergence of Minds & Machines”

A crisp, memorable line highlighting human–AI collaboration and interdisciplinary computing.

Theme 04

“Computing Beyond Boundaries: AI for a Sustainable Future”

Ties intelligent computing to sustainability and societal impact — strong for keynotes and special sessions.

Theme 05

“Next-Gen Intelligence: From Edge to Everywhere”

Signals edge/fog/cloud, IoT and pervasive intelligence — appealing to embedded & systems tracks.

Theme 06

“Smart Systems, Smarter Society”

Short and quotable; frames technology in terms of societal benefit and smart infrastructure.
